## Body

Your customers don't Google anymore. They ask ChatGPT or Perplexity "what's the best [your category] tool for [their use case]" and go with whatever gets named.

I wanted to know: which SaaS companies are winning that moment, and what do they have in common?

So I ran a consistent set of buying-intent prompts across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews for 100 named B2B SaaS brands — CRM, project management, analytics, dev tools, HR, finance — and scored each one on citation frequency, competitive share-of-voice, and context quality.

The full leaderboard is free to view at [URL]. Key findings:

- Category leaders don't always win. Several well-funded brands with strong SEO scored under 30 because their content isn't structured in a way AI models can extract and cite.
- FAQ pages and schema markup correlated more strongly with AI visibility than backlink count.
- Perplexity and ChatGPT disagree on ~40% of recommendations at the brand level.

If your brand is in the list and scored low, the leaderboard shows the specific gaps. If it's not in the list yet and you want it added, drop a comment.

## FAQ Prep

**Q1: Is this just a lead gen play to sell me something?**
> The leaderboard is genuinely free — no login, no paywall. I built a tool on top of the same engine for founders who want ongoing tracking and competitor monitoring, but the index itself is a public resource. If it's useful, bookmark it. If not, no worries.

**Q2: How do you define "AI visibility"? Isn't this just brand awareness?**
> Not quite. A brand can have massive awareness (everyone knows it) but still get skipped by AI models because the content isn't structured for extraction. We're measuring whether AI models cite you in buying-intent contexts — not whether people have heard of you. A $1M ARR startup with solid FAQ structure can outrank a $100M company on this metric.
