## Body

I've been digging into AI search visibility for the past few weeks and put together a free public leaderboard showing how 100 named B2B SaaS brands perform across the four AI platforms most likely to answer your customers' research questions: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews.

The methodology: for each brand, we ran a consistent set of category and use-case prompts ("best CRM for small teams," "top project management tools for agencies," etc.) and recorded whether the brand was mentioned, how prominently, and in what context. We scored each brand 0–100 based on citation frequency, context quality, and competitor mention share.

A few things that stood out:

- Brands with strong schema markup and FAQ pages dramatically outperformed brands with equivalent domain authority.
- Perplexity and ChatGPT diverge significantly on which brands they surface — running SEO for one doesn't guarantee the other.
- Several $10M+ ARR companies scored under 20. Brand awareness ≠ AI visibility.

## FAQ Prep

**Q1: What prompts did you use? This feels like it could be gamed easily.**
> Good question — prompt selection is the hardest part of this. We used 25 category-level and job-to-be-done prompts per brand vertical (e.g., "best [category] tools for [use case]") rather than branded queries. The full prompt set is listed in the methodology section of the leaderboard. You're right that brands could target these specific prompts, but the underlying patterns — schema, FAQ depth, third-party citation density — are harder to game than traditional SEO signals, and optimizing for them tends to generalize.

**Q2: How is this different from just tracking mentions with a brand monitoring tool?**
> Brand monitors track whether you're mentioned somewhere on the web. This tracks whether AI models cite you when a potential customer asks a buying-intent question. The difference is context — a mention in a negative comparison doesn't help you, but being cited first in "what's the best [X] for [use case]" does. We're tracking citation position, sentiment framing, and share-of-voice against direct competitors, not raw mention count.

**Q3: Will you update this, or is it a one-time snapshot?**
> The plan is to re-run the scoring monthly. AI model knowledge and retrieval behavior changes — something that works today may not in 60 days. We'll publish diffs so you can see which brands are moving and why.
