## Headline

Day 0: I launched a free AI Visibility Index for 100 SaaS brands to validate demand for NeuralReach — here's the plan and what I'm tracking

---

## Body

I'm building NeuralReach — an AI search visibility tracker for B2B SaaS founders. The pitch: your customers now ask ChatGPT or Perplexity "what tool should I use" before they ever visit your website. Most SaaS brands have no idea whether they're being cited in those answers, or what it would take to show up.

To validate this before charging for anything, I built a free public leaderboard: the AI Visibility Index. It scores 100 named B2B SaaS brands on how they appear in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews, using 25 buying-intent prompts per category. Each brand gets a 0–100 score based on citation frequency, position, and share-of-voice against competitors.

**Why the leaderboard first:**
Peec.ai hit $4M ARR in 10 months in this space. Otterly and Gracker are both early but have paying customers. The market exists. What I don't know is whether my specific distribution strategy works. The leaderboard is a falsifiable test: if I can't get 500 visits and 50 waitlist signups by day 14, I'll pivot to a paid-listing directory using the same infrastructure.

**What I'm measuring:**
- Visits to the leaderboard page
- Waitlist signups (conversion rate from visit)
- Inbound messages from low-ranked brands
- LinkedIn connection requests from cold outbound to bottom-ranked brands

**The honest constraints:**
I'm building this as a solo founder under NeuralMedic. 30-day runway to first paying customers. Pricing: $39/mo Starter, $89/mo Pro. Target: 3–6 customers in 30 days.

The leaderboard is live at [URL]. Feedback on the scoring methodology especially welcome — this community has seen a lot of "I built a leaderboard" launches, and I want this one to actually be useful as a research artifact, not just a hook.

Will post updates as the distribution experiment plays out.

## FAQ Prep

**Q1: Why not just launch the paid product directly? Why the free leaderboard first?**
> Two reasons. First, cold outreach to "pay me $39/month to track your AI visibility" is a hard sell with no social proof. Cold outreach to "your brand scored 18/100 on the AI Visibility Index — want to know why?" is a specific problem statement. The leaderboard creates the conversation opener. Second, I genuinely don't know if enough founders feel pain around this yet. The leaderboard tells me that within two weeks instead of two months of building.

**Q2: What's your biggest concern with the plan?**
> Honestly, that "AI visibility" is still too abstract for most founders to feel urgency about. My mitigation is framing everything in competitive terms — it's easier to care when you see a specific competitor outranking you. The leaderboard design puts your competitors right next to you in the same table.

**Q3: How are you handling the prompt methodology — won't brands just optimize for your specific prompts?**
> Yes, and that's fine. The prompts are public. Optimizing for them means improving FAQ structure, schema markup, and third-party citation patterns — which are exactly the behaviors that improve AI visibility generally. If brands start gaming the specific prompts, I update the prompt set. It's a moving target by design, which is also why a monthly subscription makes sense: the score you have today won't hold forever.
